üé™ GUIDED EXAMPLES

Example 1: Diego's World Cup Schedule üèÜ

Diego lives in New York and wants to watch a World Cup match happening in Brazil. The match starts at 3:00 PM in Brazil. Brazil is 1 hour ahead of New York. What time will Diego watch the match?

Step-by-Step Solution:

  1. Brazil time: 3:00 PM
  2. Brazil is 1 hour AHEAD of New York
  3. So New York is 1 hour BEHIND Brazil
  4. \( 3:00 \text{ PM} - 1 \text{ hour} = 2:00 \text{ PM} \)
  5. Answer: Diego watches at 2:00 PM ‚öΩ

Example 2: Champions League Double-Header üåü

Two Champions League matches are scheduled:

  • Match 1: 2:45 PM
  • Match 2: 5:00 PM

How much time is between the matches?

Step-by-Step Solution:

  1. End time: 5:00 PM
  2. Start time: 2:45 PM
  3. \( 5:00 \text{ PM} - 2:45 \text{ PM} \)
  4. To subtract, borrow an hour: \( 4:60 \text{ PM} - 2:45 \text{ PM} = 2:15 \)
  5. Answer: 2 hours and 15 minutes between matches ‚öΩ

Example 3: Maria's International Tournament üåç

Maria is organizing matches across 4 time zones:

  • London: 7:00 PM
  • Berlin (1 hour east): ?
  • Moscow (2 hours east of Berlin): ?
  • Tokyo (6 hours east of Moscow): ?

Step-by-Step Solution:

  1. London: 7:00 PM (base time)
  2. Berlin: \( 7:00 \text{ PM} + 1 \text{ hour} = 8:00 \text{ PM} \)
  3. Moscow: \( 8:00 \text{ PM} + 2 \text{ hours} = 10:00 \text{ PM} \)
  4. Tokyo: \( 10:00 \text{ PM} + 6 \text{ hours} = 4:00 \text{ AM (next day)} \)

Answers: Berlin: 8:00 PM, Moscow: 10:00 PM, Tokyo: 4:00 AM üïê

  • New York: 4:00 PM

Problem 5:

Diego's tournament schedule shows:

  • Match A: 1:30 PM - 3:15 PM
  • Match B: 4:00 PM - 5:45 PM
  • Match C: 7:15 PM - 9:00 PM

How much total break time is there between all matches?

Answer: Break 1 (3:15 to 4:00) is 45 mins. Break 2 (5:45 to 7:15) is 1 hr 30 mins (90 mins). Total: \( 45 + 90 = 135 \text{ minutes} \), or 2 hours and 15 minutes. ‚öΩ

Problem 6:

A Champions League match starts simultaneously in Madrid, Paris, and Rome at 9:00 PM. It's shown at 3:00 PM in New York, 12:00 PM in Los Angeles, and 5:00 AM (next day) in Tokyo. If the match lasts 1 hour 50 minutes, create a complete end-time schedule for all cities.

Answer: End times: Madrid 10:50 PM, Paris 10:50 PM, Rome 10:50 PM, New York 4:50 PM, Los Angeles 1:50 PM, Tokyo 6:50 AM (next day). ‚öΩ

Problem 7:

Elena is a soccer coach scheduling international friendlies. Berlin is 1 hour ahead of London, and Moscow is 2 hours ahead of Berlin. What 2-hour window works for all three cities? Give the time in each city.

Answer: The 2-hour window is 4:00 PM-6:00 PM London time. This corresponds to 5:00 PM-7:00 PM in Berlin and 7:00 PM-9:00 PM in Moscow. ‚öΩ

Challenge 1 (Problem 8): The Trans-Atlantic Tryout ‚úàÔ∏è

A young player from Los Angeles (LA) is flying to a tryout in London. Her flight leaves LA at 8:00 PM on Friday. The flight is 10 hours and 30 minutes long. London is 8 hours ahead of LA. After landing, she has a 2-hour and 15-minute layover before taking a 1-hour taxi to the stadium. What day and time (in London time) will she arrive at the stadium?

Answer: She will arrive at 5:45 PM on Saturday in London.

Steps: Flight lands at 6:30 AM Saturday (LA time). Convert to London time: 6:30 AM + 8 hours = 2:30 PM London time. Add layover and taxi: \( 2:30 \text{ PM} + 2h 15m + 1h = 5:45 \text{ PM} \). üèÜ

Challenge 2 (Problem 9): The Coaches' Conference Call üíª

Three coaches need a conference call. They are in Tokyo (Available 8:00 AM to 12:00 PM), Berlin (Available 1:00 AM to 5:00 AM), and New York (Available 6:00 PM to 10:00 PM the previous day). Tokyo is 7 hours ahead of Berlin, and Berlin is 6 hours ahead of New York. What is the longest possible duration for their call, and during which time window (in Berlin time) can they all meet?

Answer: The longest call is 3 hours, between 1:00 AM and 4:00 AM Berlin time.

Steps: Convert all windows to Berlin time. Tokyo (8 AM-12 PM) becomes 1 AM-5 AM Berlin time. New York (6 PM-10 PM previous day) becomes 12 AM-4 AM Berlin time. The common overlapping window for all three is 1:00 AM to 4:00 AM. üèÜ

Challenge 3 (Problem 10): The Round-Robin Relay üèÖ

A mini-tournament is held in Rio de Janeiro. The tournament starts at 9:00 AM in Rio. Each game lasts 1 hour and 30 minutes, with a 30-minute break between each game. There are 3 games in total. A fan in Los Angeles wants to know when the final whistle of the last game will blow in her local time. (Rio is 5 hours ahead of Los Angeles.)

Answer: The final whistle will be at 9:30 AM in Los Angeles.

Steps: Each game + break takes 2 hours. Game 1 (9:00-10:30), Game 2 (11:00-12:30), Game 3 (1:00-2:30). The last game ends at 2:30 PM Rio time. Convert to LA time: \( 2:30 \text{ PM} - 5 \text{ hours} = 9:30 \text{ AM} \). üèÜ
