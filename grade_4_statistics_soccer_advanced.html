üåü GUIDED EXAMPLES

Example 1: Historical Ticket Price Comparison üìú‚öΩ

In 1994, Roberto Baggio's World Cup final ticket cost $50. Today's Champions League final tickets cost $500. Diego wants to understand this amazing price jump!

Step 1: Find the difference between old and new prices
\[\text{Price Difference} = \$500 - \$50 = \$450\]
Step 2: Find how many times more expensive today's tickets are
\[\text{Price Multiple} = \$500 \div \$50 = 10 \text{ times}\]
Answer: Today's tickets cost $450 more and are 10 times more expensive! ü§Ø

Example 2: Strategic Savings Planning üí™‚öΩ

Diego earns $5 per week helping his dad organize soccer equipment. He wants to save for a $100 premium season pass to watch his local team practice and play.

Step 1: Calculate total weeks needed
\[\text{Weeks Needed} = \$100 \div \$5 = 20 \text{ weeks}\]
Step 2: Convert to months for better understanding
\[20 \text{ weeks} \div 4 \text{ weeks per month} = 5 \text{ months}\]
Answer: Diego needs exactly 20 weeks (5 months) of saving! ‚≠ê

Example 3: Tournament Seating Strategy üèÜ‚öΩ

At the youth soccer championship, there are three seating options:

  • General Admission: $25
  • Premium Seats: $75
  • VIP Box Seats: $125

Diego's family of 4 wants to compare their total costs for different seating choices.

Step 1: Calculate cost for each option
  • General: \(4 \times \$25 = \$100\)
  • Premium: \(4 \times \$75 = \$300\)
  • VIP: \(4 \times \$125 = \$500\)
Step 2: Find the differences
  • Premium vs General: \(\$300 - \$100 = \$200\)
  • VIP vs Premium: \(\$500 - \$300 = \$200\)
Answer: Each upgrade costs exactly $200 more for the family! üí°

üöÄ INDEPENDENT PRACTICE PROBLEMS

Problem 1: ‚öΩ Youth League Economics

Maria's soccer team is planning to attend a professional match. Adult tickets cost $45 each, and student tickets cost $25 each. If 6 adults and 15 students attend, what is the total cost?

Solution:
Adult cost: \(6 \times \$45 = \$270\)
Student cost: \(15 \times \$25 = \$375\)
Total: \(\$270 + \$375 = \$645\)

Problem 2: üé´ Tournament Package Deal

The soccer tournament offers a family package: 4 tickets plus 4 meals for $280. Individual tickets cost $55 each and meals cost $15 each. How much money does the family package save?

Solution:
Individual cost: \((4 \times \$55) + (4 \times \$15) = \$220 + \$60 = \$280\)
Savings: \(\$280 - \$280 = \$0\) (No savings - same price!)

Problem 3: ‚è∞ Savings Goal Challenge

Carlos wants to attend 8 different soccer matches this season. Each ticket costs $35. If he saves $12 per week from his allowance, how many weeks will it take him to save enough money?

Solution:
Total needed: \(8 \times \$35 = \$280\)
Weeks needed: \(\$280 \div \$12 = 23.33...\) ‚Üí 24 weeks (He needs to save for the full 24th week)

Problem 4: üèüÔ∏è Stadium Capacity Planning

The new soccer stadium has 3 sections:

  • North Stand: 2,400 seats at $30 each
  • East Stand: 1,800 seats at $50 each
  • West Premium: 600 seats at $80 each

If all seats are sold, what is the total revenue for the stadium?

Solution:
North: \(2,400 \times \$30 = \$72,000\)
East: \(1,800 \times \$50 = \$90,000\)
West: \(600 \times \$80 = \$48,000\)
Total: \(\$72,000 + \$90,000 + \$48,000 = \$210,000\)

üî• ADVANCED CHALLENGE PROBLEMS

Challenge 1: üß† Family Budget Strategy

Diego's family of 4 has a total budget of $450 for a tournament day. The budget must cover tickets, $20 for parking, a family meal deal for $70, and one souvenir per person at $25 each. Using the seating options from Guided Example 3 (General: $25, Premium: $75, VIP: $125), which is the most expensive seating option they can afford for the whole family without going over budget? Show your calculations.

Solution:
1. Calculate fixed costs: Parking ($20) + Meal ($70) + Souvenirs \((4 \times \$25 = \$100)\) = $190.
2. Calculate money left for tickets: \(\$450 - \$190 = \$260\).
3. Test seating options for 4 people:
- VIP: \(4 \times \$125 = \$500\) (Too expensive)
- Premium: \(4 \times \$75 = \$300\) (Too expensive)
- General: \(4 \times \$25 = \$100\) (Affordable)
Answer: The most expensive option they can afford is General Admission.

Challenge 2: üéØ Season Pass Optimization

A club offers two season passes for its 20 home games:

  • Gold Pass: $320 (access to all 20 games)
  • Silver Pass: $200 (access to any 10 games)

Single game tickets cost $25 each.

A family of 4 plans to attend 14 games together. What is the cheapest way for the family to attend all 14 games? Compare the cost of buying all single tickets vs. buying 4 Silver Passes and single tickets for the extra games.

Solution:
1. Cost with all single tickets: \(4 \text{ people} \times 14 \text{ games} \times \$25/\text{ticket} = \$1,400\).
2. Cost with Silver Passes + extra games:
- Cost of 4 Silver Passes: \(4 \times \$200 = \$800\). This covers 10 games.
- They need to attend 4 more games \((14 - 10)\).
- Cost of extra games: \(4 \text{ people} \times 4 \text{ games} \times \$25/\text{ticket} = \$400\).
- Total cost: \(\$800 + \$400 = \$1,200\).
Answer: The cheapest way is to buy 4 Silver Passes and single tickets for the other 4 games, for a total cost of $1,200.

Challenge 3: üèÜ Tournament Bracket Economics

In a knockout tournament with 16 teams, ticket prices increase by $15 after each round:

  • Round 1 (8 games): $25 per ticket
  • Round 2 (4 games): $40 per ticket
  • Round 3 (2 games): $55 per ticket
  • Final (1 game): $70 per ticket

If each game averages 5,000 spectators, what is the total revenue for the entire tournament?

Solution:
Round 1: \(8 \times 5,000 \times \$25 = \$1,000,000\)
Round 2: \(4 \times 5,000 \times \$40 = \$800,000\)
Round 3: \(2 \times 5,000 \times \$55 = \$550,000\)
Final: \(1 \times 5,000 \times \$70 = \$350,000\)
Total: \(\$1,000,000 + \$800,000 + \$550,000 + \$350,000 = \$2,700,000\)

üéä BONUS BRAIN TEASER

Diego notices that his piggy bank has only quarters ($0.25) and he counted exactly 148 coins. His sister's bank has only dimes ($0.10) but contains exactly $37.00. Who has more money, and by how much? ü§îüí∞

Solution:
Diego: \(148 \times \$0.25 = \$37.00\)
Sister: $37.00
They have exactly the same amount! It's a tie! üéâ
