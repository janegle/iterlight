üìñ Vocabulary and Key Concepts

üî¢ Vector: A quantity possessing both magnitude and direction. It can be represented as \( \vec{v} = \langle v_x, v_y \ angle \) in component form.
üìä Scalar: A quantity possessing only magnitude (e.g., speed, distance, time).
üìè Magnitude: The length or size of a vector. For \( \vec{v} = \langle v_x, v_y \ angle \), the magnitude is \( \|\vec{v}\| = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2} \).
üîÑ Relative Velocity: The velocity of an object with respect to a particular frame of reference. The core equation for this lesson is:
\[ \vec{v}_{A/C} = \vec{v}_{A/B} + \vec{v}_{B/C} \]
Where:
  • \( \vec{v}_{A/C} \) is the velocity of object A relative to frame C (e.g., Dancer relative to Audience).
  • \( \vec{v}_{A/B} \) is the velocity of object A relative to frame B (e.g., Dancer relative to Stage).
  • \( \vec{v}_{B/C} \) is the velocity of frame B relative to frame C (e.g., Stage relative to Audience).
‚≠ï Angular Velocity (œâ): The rate of rotation, typically measured in radians per second.
‚û°Ô∏è Tangential Velocity (\(\vec{v}_t\)): The linear velocity of a point on a rotating object. Its magnitude is \( v_t = r\omega \), where r is the distance from the center of rotation. Its direction is always tangent to the circular path.

üéØ Critical Thinking Example 1: The Basic "Eight-Count"

A young dancer, Leo üíÉ, is practicing a simple combination. His choreographer tells him to move from the center of the stage, which we'll define as the origin (0,0), to a mark at position (4, 3) meters. He must complete this movement in exactly 4 seconds (a standard "eight-count" in music). What is Leo's required constant velocity vector? Analyze how changes in time or position would affect the model.

Solution:

1. Identify the displacement vector. The displacement, \( \Delta\vec{p} \), is the change in position from the start point to the end point.

\[ \Delta\vec{p} = \langle 4 - 0, 3 - 0 \ angle = \langle 4, 3 \ angle \text{ meters} \]

2. Use the velocity formula. Velocity is displacement divided by time.

\[ \vec{v} = \frac{\Delta\vec{p}}{\Delta t} = \frac{\langle 4, 3 \ angle}{4} = \langle 1, 0.75 \ angle \text{ m/s} \]

3. Conclusion and Analysis: Leo must maintain \( \langle 1, 0.75 \ angle \) m/s. If time increases, velocity decreases proportionally; justify why this models real choreography constraints.

The musical "Hamilton" üé≠ uses a turntable with radius 7 meters rotating counter-clockwise at \( \omega = \frac{\pi}{12} \) rad/s. A dancer at (0, 7) stands still on the stage. Find the dancer's velocity relative to the audience. (AMC-style: Compute exact vector.)

üé™ Example 3: Maya's Choreographic Challenge

Maya wants a dancer üï∫ to appear to glide from (-6, 2) to (6, 2) in 8 seconds on a stage of radius 5 m rotating at \( \omega = \frac{\pi}{16} \) rad/s. At (3, 4), find \( \vec{v}_{D/S} \).
